Automating ELISA assay analysis at Merck KGaA

Experimental biologists at Merck were spending hours manually copying assay data from electronic lab notebooks into Excel and GraphPad for every experiment. The process was slow, error-prone, and impossible to scale as LNP research volumes grew. I built a Python pipeline that pulls data directly from the Revvity ELN API, runs curve fitting and metric extraction automatically, and writes results back — replacing a multi-step manual workflow with a single command.

One-click execution replaced hours of manual analysis per experiment

Background

Built at the intersection of business and data.

I started my career in commercial operations at Merck KGaA, where I quickly became the person who built the analyses and tools that others relied on. Alongside my work, I completed an MSc in Data Science and Society at Tilburg University, with a thesis on machine learning and fairness evaluation applied to Dutch public health data.

I'm now building toward independent data work — taking on clearly scoped projects where I can deliver end-to-end, own the outcome, and work across domains and industries.
